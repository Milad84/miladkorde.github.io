Biography

An experienced GIS scientist, professor, and sustainability geographer. Highly skilled in collecting, processing, editing, and manipulating geospatial data. I use geo-statistics to extract insight from data. Experienced in financial analysis, managing geodatabases using Excel, SQL, PostgreSQL, PostGIS, Python, and ESRI-based software. Currently, I am teaching at Clark University, where I work as a Visiting Assistant Professor and at Northwest Missouri State University as an Adjunct Professor. I have taught Geographic Information Science at introductory, intermediate, and advanced levels in different frameworks such as web mapping, PostgreSQL, QGIS, and R. I have also used and taught APIs in Geospatial studies applying different languages like Javascript and Python. I am familiar with five languages and can speak fluently 3 of them: Persian, my native language, English, and Italian. I know a little of Spanish and Arabic as well. I lived in Italy for nine years, where I got my bachelor’s degree and my first master’s degree in business communication. I love teaching, researching, and solving data science problems using statistics, programming, and geography. For more information, check my CV, LinkedIn, or my Weblog.
